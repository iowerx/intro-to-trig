    Concept Taxonomy

    Trigonometric Functions Course - Category System

    This taxonomy organizes the 200 concepts into 12 meaningful categories that support navigation, assessment, and learning pathways.

    Category Definitions

    1. Foundational Geometry (GEOM)

    Concept Count: 26 concepts (13%) Description: Basic geometric principles including triangle types, properties, angles, and classifications. These concepts form the prerequisite foundation for all trigonometric understanding. Key Concepts: Right triangles, angle types, triangle classification, angle measurement, properties of angles Why Important: Students must understand basic geometric relationships before engaging with trigonometric ratios.

    2. Pythagorean Foundation (PYTH)

    Concept Count: 16 concepts (8%) Description: The Pythagorean theorem and its applications, including proofs, triples, verification methods, and foundational problem-solving with the theorem. Key Concepts: Theorem statement, proof, triples, verification, converse, applications Why Important: The Pythagorean theorem is fundamental to trigonometry and bridges geometry and trigonometric problem-solving.

    3. Trigonometric Ratios (RATIO)

    Concept Count: 35 concepts (17.5%) Description: Core trigonometric ratios (sine, cosine, tangent) and reciprocal functions. Includes SOHCAHTOA mnemonic, ratio selection, and application in right triangles. Key Concepts: Sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant, cotangent, ratio formulas, opposite/adjacent/hypotenuse Why Important: Trigonometric ratios are the central concept of the entire course; all applications depend on understanding these ratios.

    4. Special Angles & Values (SPECIAL)

    Concept Count: 14 concepts (7%) Description: Special right triangles (30-60-90 and 45-45-90) and trigonometric values of special angles. Quick reference values without calculator. Key Concepts: Special triangles, special angle ratios, sine/cosine/tangent of 30/45/60 degrees Why Important: Students should memorize special angle values for efficiency in problem-solving and for understanding patterns.

    5. Unit Circle & Trigonometric Functions (CIRCLE)

    Concept Count: 30 concepts (15%) Description: Unit circle definition, angles in standard position, trigonometric functions as mappings, domains, ranges, and graphical representations. Key Concepts: Unit circle, standard position, terminal side, trigonometric functions, domains, ranges, graphs Why Important: The unit circle extends trigonometry beyond right triangles and provides foundation for periodic behavior and advanced applications.

    6. Trigonometric Identities (IDENTITY)

    Concept Count: 15 concepts (7.5%) Description: Fundamental trigonometric identities including Pythagorean identities, quotient identities, reciprocal identities, and angle formulas. Key Concepts: Pythagorean identity, quotient identity, reciprocal identity, even-odd identities, cofunction identities, angle addition/difference formulas, double angle formulas Why Important: Identities are tools for simplifying expressions and solving complex trigonometric equations.

    7. Inverse & Equation Solving (INVERSE)

    Concept Count: 27 concepts (13.5%) Description: Inverse trigonometric functions and solving trigonometric equations. Includes domain/range restrictions, angle finding, and equation solution methods. Key Concepts: Inverse functions (arcsin, arccos, arctan), angle calculation, equation solving, inequality solving, general and particular solutions Why Important: Essential for finding unknown angles and solving real-world problems where angles must be determined from ratios.

    8. Advanced Triangle Solutions (ADVANCED)

    Concept Count: 28 concepts (14%) Description: Laws of sines and cosines, triangle area formulas, and methods for solving non-right triangles. Key Concepts: Law of sines, law of cosines, ambiguous case, triangle area formulas, Heron's formula Why Important: These methods extend trigonometry to all triangles, not just right triangles, enabling broader problem-solving.

    9. Real-World Applications (APPLY)

    Concept Count: 26 concepts (13%) Description: Practical applications including navigation, surveying, engineering, architecture, and physics. Height/distance problems, bearing problems, and real-world scenario modeling. Key Concepts: Navigation, bearing, surveying, indirect measurement, engineering applications, architecture, physics applications Why Important: Demonstrates relevance of trigonometry to students and motivates learning through real-world contexts.

    10. Trigonometric Tools (TOOLS)

    Concept Count: 11 concepts (5.5%) Description: Tools for trigonometric work including calculators, tables, formulas references, and computational skills. Key Concepts: Calculator usage, degree/radian modes, trigonometric tables, formulas reference, significant figures, error analysis Why Important: Practical skills for efficient problem-solving and accuracy in calculations.

    11. Harmonic Motion & Waves (WAVE)

    Concept Count: 9 concepts (4.5%) Description: Applications to periodic phenomena, wave motion, harmonic oscillation, and frequency. Connects trigonometry to physics. Key Concepts: Wave motion, amplitude, period, frequency, harmonic motion, oscillation, periodic phenomena Why Important: Trigonometric functions naturally model oscillatory behavior, connecting to physics and real-world phenomena.

    12. Integration & Synthesis (SYNTH)

    Concept Count: 3 concepts (1.5%) Description: Summary and integration concepts that bring together the entire course. Final synthesis of learning. Key Concepts: Comprehensive identities summary, trigonometric formulas reference, integration of all knowledge areas Why Important: Helps students consolidate learning and see connections across the course.

    Category Sequencing

    The taxonomy supports both hierarchical and sequential learning paths:

    Foundational Path

    GEOM → PYTH → RATIO → CIRCLE → IDENTITY → INVERSE → ADVANCED

    Application Path

    RATIO → INVERSE → APPLY → WAVE

    Comprehensive Path

    GEOM → PYTH → RATIO → SPECIAL → CIRCLE → IDENTITY → INVERSE → ADVANCED → APPLY → TOOLS → WAVE → SYNTH

    Notes on Distribution

    • Well-Balanced Categories: No category exceeds 20% of total concepts
    • Clear Progression: Categories flow from foundational to advanced
    • Application-Focused: 13% of concepts are directly applied (APPLY) with additional 4.5% for waves
    • Tool Support: 5.5% dedicated to computational tools and references
    • Synthesis: Final 1.5% for integration and summary

    This taxonomy supports multiple learning pathways while maintaining pedagogical coherence.
